\caption[Herd-immunity threshold versus $R_0$]{The herd-immunity
threshold $p_c = 1 - 1/R_0$ as a function of $R_0$. Pathogens with
$R_0 < 1$ die out without intervention; pathogens with $R_0 > 1$
sustain spread until the susceptible pool drops to $N/R_0$. The
threshold rises sharply at low $R_0$ and flattens at high values.
$R_0$ values are conditional on contact structure in the population
at the time of measurement; the data points are mid-range
estimates for the named pathogens.}
\label{fig:vol06:ch08:r0-regions}
