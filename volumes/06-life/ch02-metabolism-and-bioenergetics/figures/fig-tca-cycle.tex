\caption[The TCA cycle ledger]{The eight-enzyme tricarboxylic-acid
cycle in the mitochondrial matrix. Each acetyl-CoA entering the cycle
yields three NADH, one FADH$_2$, one GTP (equivalent to one ATP), and
two CO$_2$. Two CO$_2$ are released per turn: one at isocitrate
dehydrogenase and one at $\alpha$-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase. Per
original glucose (which produces two acetyl-CoA via pyruvate
dehydrogenase), the cycle yields six NADH, two FADH$_2$, two GTP, and
four CO$_2$. Plus the two NADH from pyruvate dehydrogenase, plus the
two NADH and two ATP from cytosolic glycolysis: ten NADH, two
FADH$_2$, four ATP (substrate-level), and six CO$_2$ per glucose
before oxidative phosphorylation begins.}
