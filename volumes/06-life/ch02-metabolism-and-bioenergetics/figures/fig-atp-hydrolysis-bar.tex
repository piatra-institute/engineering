\caption[ATP hydrolysis in context]{Standard free energies of
hydrolysis for biological phosphate compounds at pH 7.0, 310~K, unit
activity. ATP at $\Delta G^{\circ\prime} = -30.5$~kJ/mol sits in the
middle of the high-energy phosphate range. Cellular ATP, at the far-
from-equilibrium ratio that the mitochondrial machinery maintains, has
an effective $\Delta G$ of approximately $-55$~kJ/mol (range $-50$ to
$-60$). The intermediate position lets ATP phosphorylate the lower-
energy compounds (glycerol, glucose-6-phosphate) and be regenerated by
the higher-energy compounds (phosphoenolpyruvate (PEP), creatine
phosphate, 1,3-bisphosphoglycerate (1,3-BPG)). Values after Lehninger
\cite[table~13.1]{text:lehninger-biochemistry}.}
