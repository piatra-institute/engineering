\caption[Z-scheme of oxygenic photosynthesis]{Reduction-potential
diagram of oxygenic photosynthesis. The vertical axis is the standard
reduction potential $E^{\circ\prime}$; the horizontal axis is the
chain order. Two photons (amber arrows) drive uphill excitations: at
PSII (P680 absorbs a 680-nm photon) and at PSI (P700 absorbs a 700-nm
photon). Between the photosystems, electrons flow downhill through
plastoquinone (PQ), the cytochrome $b_6 f$ complex, and plastocyanin
(PC), pumping protons into the thylakoid lumen and driving chloroplast
ATP synthesis. After PSI the electrons reduce ferredoxin (Fd) and
finally NADP$^+$ to NADPH. The overall path traces a Z-shape on the
energy axis, hence the name. Water at the PSII entry is the electron
donor: 2 H$_2$O give 4 e$^-$, 4 H$^+$, and O$_2$. After Nelson and Cox
\cite[fig.~19-49]{text:lehninger-biochemistry}.}
\label{fig:vol06:ch02:z-scheme}
