\caption{Batch-failure cause tree for biomanufacturing. The four
primary causes of batch rejection are contamination, scale-up loss,
quality-system or record failure, and cell-line drift. Each branches
into three representative leaf mechanisms; the leaves are not
exhaustive. Note that the four primary causes are not mutually
exclusive: a scale-up oxygen-transfer deficit can drive the production
strain into a metabolic state that selects for non-producers, and a
contamination event can be detected only through an out-of-specification
analytical result. The reading discipline is that a rejected batch is
both a material loss and an information loss, and the root-cause
investigation cannot end at the most visible symptom.}
