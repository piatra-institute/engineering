\caption{Volumetric oxygen-transfer coefficient $k_L a$ as a function
of bioreactor working volume on log-log axes, for three scale-up rules.
Constant geometric similarity (red) preserves vessel shape and impeller
geometry but lets $k_L a$ fall by an order of magnitude or more from
1\,L to 10000\,L because tip speed must rise to hold mixing intensity
and the resulting shear and heat are unacceptable. Constant
power-per-volume (orange) is a common compromise. Holding $k_L a$
constant (blue) requires raising power input per volume, gas flow, and
sometimes operating pressure as the vessel grows, and is the only one
of the three that protects oxygen-limited aerobic fermentation directly.
The shaded band marks the $k_L a$ range typical of aerobic microbial
demand at standard cell densities; the geometric-similarity curve runs
below it at production scale, which is why an unmodified scale-up of an
oxygen-demanding process will fail.}
\label{fig:vol06ch10:kla-scale-up}
