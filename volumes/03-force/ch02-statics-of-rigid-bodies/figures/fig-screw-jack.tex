\caption[Screw jack and the unwrapped thread]{A square-thread screw jack.
A hand force $F$ on a handle of length $a$ turns the screw of mean thread
radius $r$ and pitch $p$ against an axial load $W$. Unwrapping a single turn
of the thread (right) turns the screw into an inclined plane of lead angle
$\lambda = \arctan\!\big(p/(2\pi r)\big)$: the load $W$ is a block that the
tangential driving force $F_t = M/r$ pushes up the incline against gravity
and friction. The screw's large mechanical advantage comes from the small
lead angle, which lets a light handle force raise a heavy load, and the same
small angle makes the jack self-locking so the load does not run the screw
back down.}
\label{fig:vol03ch02:screw-jack}
\end{figure}
