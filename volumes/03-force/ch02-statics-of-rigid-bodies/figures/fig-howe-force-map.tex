\caption[Solved member-force map of the case-study Howe truss]{The solved
member-force map of the case-study Howe truss, with $P=10\,\text{kN}$ at
each top-chord joint. Tension members are drawn in green, compression in
red, and the two zero-force members ($T_1L_1$ and $T_7L_7$) as dashed
gray. The map is symmetric about the ridge, so only the left half carries
labels; forces are in kilonewtons. The bottom chord runs in tension and
grows from $50\,\text{kN}$ near the ridge to $70\,\text{kN}$ at the
supports, while the rafters run in compression and grow the other way, to
$78.3\,\text{kN}$ at the end panel next to each support. The governing
members are therefore the two end rafters ($78.3\,\text{kN}$ compression,
buckling-critical for their unbraced length) and the four support-adjacent
bottom-chord panels ($70\,\text{kN}$ tension, net-section critical at
their splices).}
\label{fig:vol03ch02:howe-force-map}
\end{figure}
