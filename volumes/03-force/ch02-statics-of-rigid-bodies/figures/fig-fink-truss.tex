\caption[Symmetric Fink roof truss]{A symmetric Fink roof truss of span
$L=12\,\si{\meter}$ rising to a ridge $h=3\,\si{\meter}$ above the supports.
The Fink web subdivides each rafter with a pair of members so that the long
rafters are broken into shorter unbraced lengths against buckling, the reason
the Fink pattern is the standard timber and light-steel roof truss for medium
spans. Equal purlin loads $P$ hang at the three upper panel points $T_1$,
$T_2$, $T_3$. The left support is a pin and the right a roller, three
reactions for a statically determinate truss. The symmetry of the geometry
and the loading halves the work: the two reactions are equal, and the
member forces on the right mirror those on the left, so resolving the left
half resolves the whole.}
\label{fig:vol03ch02:fink-truss}
