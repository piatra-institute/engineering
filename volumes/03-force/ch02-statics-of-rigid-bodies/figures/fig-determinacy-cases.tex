\caption[The determinacy classification on three beams]{The reaction
count against the three planar equilibrium equations classifies a body
before any force is solved. The single-pin beam at left has two reactions
against three equations and is free to rotate about the pin: a mechanism,
with no equilibrium solution for a general load. The pin-and-roller beam
at centre has exactly three reactions and is statically determinate, with
a unique solution. The pin-and-two-roller beam at right has four
reactions against three equations and is statically indeterminate to the
first degree: equilibrium alone leaves one reaction undetermined, and the
beam's own bending stiffness, the subject of chapter 8, picks the value.
The count is necessary but not sufficient; the determinate centre case
also requires that its three reaction lines be neither all concurrent nor
all parallel.}
