\caption[Shear legs as a three-dimensional joint]{Shear legs, an A-frame
lifting device: two spread legs meet at an apex from which a load $W$ hangs,
and a back-stay runs down to a ground anchor. The apex is a single point in
equilibrium under four concurrent forces, the two leg compressions, the
back-stay tension $T$, and the load, so its three force-balance equations
resolve the three member forces at once. The plan view (right) shows the legs
spread by a half-angle $\psi$ either side of the vertical plane through the
back-stay; the spread shares the load between the two legs and stabilises the
frame against sideways motion. Widening the spread lowers each leg's share of
a sideways load at the cost of a longer leg for the same lift height.}
\label{fig:vol03ch02:shear-legs}
\end{figure}
