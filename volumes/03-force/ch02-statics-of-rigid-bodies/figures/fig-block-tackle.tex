\caption[Block and tackle]{A block and tackle with a fixed upper block and a
moving lower block, rigged with $n=4$ rope falls supporting the load. An ideal
(frictionless) rope carries the same tension $T$ in every fall, so the free
body of the moving block (right) balances $nT$ up against the load $W$ down,
giving $T = W/n$ and an input pull of $W/n$ at the free end. The ideal
mechanical advantage equals the number of falls supporting the moving block,
the count read straight off the rigging. Real tackle falls short of the ideal
because each sheave adds a friction loss the capstan relation quantifies, so
the pull needed exceeds $W/n$ by the accumulated sheave friction.}
\label{fig:vol03ch02:block-tackle}
\end{figure}
