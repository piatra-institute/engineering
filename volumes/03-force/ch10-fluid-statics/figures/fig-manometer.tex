\caption[Open-end U-tube manometer]{An open-end U-tube manometer. The
left leg connects to a gas line at unknown pressure $p_{\mathrm{conn}}$;
the right leg is open to the atmosphere. The denser manometer fluid
(blue) stands higher in the open leg by $\Delta h$. Tracing the
hydrostatic path from the gas surface down one leg and up the other and
neglecting the light gas column gives the gauge pressure
$p_{\mathrm{conn,gauge}}=\rho_m g\,\Delta h$. The discipline is to trace
the path, adding $\rho g\,\Delta z$ on every descent and subtracting it
on every rise, which fixes both the magnitude and the sign of the
reading.}
\label{fig:vol03ch10:manometer}
\end{figure}
