\caption[Well-type cistern manometer]{A well-type manometer replaces one
leg of the U-tube with a large-area reservoir. Applied pressure $p$ pushes
the reservoir mercury down by a small drop $\delta$ and raises the narrow
tube by the reading $R$; conservation of the incompressible mercury sets
$A_r\,\delta=A_t\,R$, so the reservoir barely moves when $A_r\gg A_t$ and
a single scale on the tube gives the pressure. The exact relation is
$p_{\mathrm{gauge}}=\rho g\,(R+\delta)=\rho g\,R\,(1+A_t/A_r)$; the
area-ratio term is the small correction a precision instrument either
machines to a known value or prints onto a compressed scale so the tube
reads pressure directly.}
\label{fig:vol03ch10:well-manometer}
\end{figure}
