\caption[Octave-band SPL spectrum, unweighted and A-weighted]{Octave-band
sound-pressure-level spectrum of a representative item of rotating
machinery, before (blue) and after (red) A-weighting. The unweighted
spectrum is dominated by low-frequency content near the $63\,\si{\hertz}$
band, typical of a blade-pass or shaft-rate tone. A-weighting applies
the standardised frequency-dependent attenuation that approximates the
ear's reduced sensitivity at low frequency; it removes roughly
$40\,\si{\decibel}$ at $31.5\,\si{\hertz}$ and almost nothing near
$1\,\si{\kilo\hertz}$. The single-number A-weighted level is the
energy sum of the red bars, which is lower than the unweighted sum and
weighted toward the mid bands the ear hears best. The spectrum is read
from \texttt{data/octave\_spectrum.csv}; the weighting offsets are the
standard octave-band A values.}
\label{fig:vol03ch07:spl-octave}
