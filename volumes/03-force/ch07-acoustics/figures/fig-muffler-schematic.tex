\caption[Combined reactive and dissipative muffler]{Layout of a combined
exhaust muffler. Gas enters at the left into a reactive expansion chamber,
whose sudden area change reflects low-frequency energy back toward the
source and gives the lobed transmission loss of
figure~\ref{fig:vol03ch07:muffler-tl}; the extended inlet stub shifts the
chamber's pass-through troughs. The flow then passes through a perforated
tube wrapped in porous absorber, a dissipative section that converts
acoustic energy to heat broadband and fills the gaps the reactive chamber
leaves. The reactive element handles the strong low firing-order tones at
small back-pressure cost; the dissipative element handles the broadband
mid- and high-frequency hiss. Real exhausts chain several such elements so
that one element's pass-through frequency lands on another's peak.}
\label{fig:vol03ch07:muffler-schematic}
