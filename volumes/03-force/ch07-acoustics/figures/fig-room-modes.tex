\caption[Cumulative room-mode count]{Cumulative count of acoustic
eigenmodes below frequency $f$ for a rigid rectangular room of
$5\times4\times2.5\,\si{\meter}$ ($V=50\,\si{\meter\cubed}$), computed
exactly (blue staircase) and compared with the leading asymptotic
estimate $N(f)\approx(4\pi/3)(V/c^3)f^3$ (red dashed). At low frequency
the modes are sparse and the staircase is jagged; individual axial and
tangential modes dominate the room response and colour the sound. As
frequency rises the count climbs as $f^3$ and the modes crowd together,
approaching the smooth Weyl estimate. The Schroeder frequency, near
where the modal overlap becomes statistical, sits in the $100$ to
$300\,\si{\hertz}$ band for a room this size; above it, statistical
room acoustics replaces modal accounting. Generated by
\texttt{code/room\_modes.py}.}
