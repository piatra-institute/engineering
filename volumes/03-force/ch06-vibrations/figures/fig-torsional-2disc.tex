\caption[Two-disc torsional oscillator and its mode]{Two rotors of
polar moments of inertia $I_1$ and $I_2$ joined by a shaft of torsional
stiffness $K$, the torsional analogue of the two-mass chain. The system
has a rigid-body rotation mode at zero frequency, in which both discs
turn together and the shaft carries no twist, and one elastic mode at
$\omega=\sqrt{K(I_1+I_2)/(I_1 I_2)}$, in which the two discs twist in
opposite senses about a stationary node, orange arrows. The node,
red dashed line, sits nearer the disc of larger inertia, at the section
that divides the shaft so that $I_1\theta_1=-I_2\theta_2$, and it is the
cross-section the shaft designer places a coupling or a torsional damper
at, where the twist amplitude is largest and the inertial motion is
zero. Engine crankshafts, ship drive lines, and turbine-generator sets
are chains of such discs, and their torsional criticals are found from
the same eigenvalue calculation extended to many discs.}
\label{fig:vol03ch06:torsional-2disc}
\end{figure}
