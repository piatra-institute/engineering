\node[label, anchor=west] at (0.1,1.5) {tap 2 ($p_2$)};

\caption[Venturi meter read by a differential manometer]{A Venturi
meter with pressure taps at the inlet and the throat connected to the
two legs of a U-tube manometer. The throat runs at a lower pressure
than the inlet, so the manometer fluid stands higher on the throat
leg; the deflection $\Delta h$ measures the pressure difference
directly as $p_1 - p_2 = (\rho_m - \rho)g\,\Delta h$, with $\rho_m$
the manometer-fluid density and $\rho$ the flowing-fluid density. The
flow rate then follows from the Venturi calibration. The manometer
turns the abstract pressure difference of the Bernoulli calculation
into a column height a person can read off a scale.}
\label{fig:vol03ch11:venturi-manometer}
\end{figure}
