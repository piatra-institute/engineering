% stream. The closed dividing streamline is a smooth oval whose
% length and thickness are set by the source-sink spacing and strength.

  ({2.2*cos(x)}, {0.9*sin(x)});
\addlegendentry{dividing streamline (oval)}

\caption[The Rankine oval from a source-sink pair in a stream]{A source
at $x=-a$ and an equal sink at $x=+a$ placed in a uniform stream close
the flux into a finite closed body: the dividing streamline is a smooth
oval, the \engterm{Rankine oval}, with a front and a rear stagnation
point. Unlike the half-body, which the source alone leaves open
downstream, the sink recaptures the source flux and the contour closes,
so the oval is a complete two-dimensional body whose length and
thickness are set by the source strength and the spacing $2a$. Bringing
the source and sink together at fixed product recovers the doublet and
the circular cylinder; spreading them apart lengthens the oval toward a
slender strut. The oval is the simplest inviscid model of a rounded
strut or a submarine hull section, and its surface pressure follows
from the superposed velocity field by Bernoulli.}
\label{fig:vol03ch11:rankine-oval}
\end{figure}
