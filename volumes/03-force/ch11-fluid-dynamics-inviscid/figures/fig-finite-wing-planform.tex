\caption[Finite wing: trailing vorticity, downwash, and induced
drag]{A finite wing carries a spanwise bound circulation $\Gamma(y)$
that must fall to zero at the tips, so vorticity is shed continuously
into a trailing sheet behind the wing. The sheet induces a downward
velocity, the downwash $w$, over the wing. The downwash tilts the
local relative wind down by the induced angle $\alpha_i$, and because
the lift acts perpendicular to the local relative wind rather than to
the freestream, a component of it points downstream: the induced
drag. Induced drag is the unavoidable price of producing lift with a
wing of finite span, and it falls as the span grows, which is why
gliders and long-range aircraft carry slender wings.}
\label{fig:vol03ch11:finite-wing-planform}
\end{figure}
