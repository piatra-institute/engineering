\caption[Intervals of convergence and endpoint verdicts]{Four power
series centred at $x_{0} = 0$, drawn as intervals of convergence on
stacked real lines. The factorial in the coefficient sets the radius:
constant coefficients give $R = 1$, a factorial in the denominator gives
$R = \infty$, and a factorial in the numerator collapses the interval to
the single point $x_{0}$. The endpoints are a separate question the
radius does not settle. The two $R = 1$ series share a radius yet differ
at the ends: $\sum x^{n}$ diverges at both $\pm 1$ (open circles), while
$\sum x^{n}/n^{2}$ converges at both (filled), because its endpoint
series is a $p$-series with $p = 2$. Reading the radius tells the
engineer how far the local expansion reaches; the endpoint test must be
run separately, term by term.}
