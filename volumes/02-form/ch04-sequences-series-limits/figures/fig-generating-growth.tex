\caption[Codebook growth and the dominant-pole asymptotic]{Payload
capacity $\log_{2} a_{n}$ of the run-length-limited code (no two adjacent
zeros), where $a_{n} = F_{n+2}$, against word length $n$. The dashed
line is the asymptotic $n\log_{2}\phi + \log_{2} C$ predicted by the
generating function's dominant pole at $x = 1/\phi$. The data join the
line within one bit by $n = 6$ and indistinguishably by $n = 12$, the
subdominant $\psi^{n}$ term having decayed. The slope is the per-symbol
capacity, $\log_{2}\phi \approx 0.694$ bits, a $31\%$ penalty against the
unconstrained $1$ bit per symbol.}
