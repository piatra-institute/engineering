\caption[The Dirichlet kernel and the Gibbs side lobe]{The Dirichlet
kernel $D_{N}(u) = \sin((N+\tfrac{1}{2})u)/\sin(u/2)$, normalised by its
peak $2N+1$, for $N = 8$. The Fourier partial sum is the signal
convolved with this kernel. As $N$ grows the main lobe narrows like
$1/N$, but the first side lobe keeps a fixed fraction of the peak, and
its area is what drives the $9\%$ Gibbs overshoot at a jump. A
non-negative kernel, the Fej\'{e}r kernel, removes the overshoot at the
cost of a wider main lobe.}
