\caption[Taylor models of a thermistor resistance about its operating
point]{Relative error of the first-order and second-order Taylor models
of a $B = 3950\,\text{K}$ NTC thermistor resistance, expanded about its
$25\,\degC$ operating point, as the temperature deviates. The linear
model holds a $1\%$ budget to about $\pm 3\,\text{K}$; adding the
quadratic term extends the same budget to roughly $\pm 8\,\text{K}$. The
slopes are the Lagrange-remainder scalings: the linear-model error grows
as $(\Delta T)^{2}$ and the quadratic-model error as $(\Delta T)^{3}$,
so each retained term steepens the error curve by one power. An engineer
sizing the sense range of the thermistor reads the truncation order
straight off the budget line, the same discipline the chapter applies to
every series truncation.}
