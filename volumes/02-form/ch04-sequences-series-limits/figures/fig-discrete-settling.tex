\caption[Geometric settling of a sampled first-order tracking loop]{Tracking
error $e_{k} = \rho^{k}$ of a sampled first-order control loop for two
loop poles, on a log scale against the sample index. The decay is a
geometric sequence, so the log-error is a straight line whose slope is
$\log\rho$, and the sample at which each line crosses the $2\%$ settling
band is the settling count: about eight samples for the fast pole
$\rho = 0.6$ and twenty-five for the slow pole $\rho = 0.85$. Halving
the distance to $\rho = 1$ roughly doubles the settling count, the
brutal cost of a pole near the unit circle. The cumulative squared
error over the whole transient is the geometric sum
$\sum_{k}\rho^{2k} = 1/(1-\rho^{2})$, the energy the loop spends
recovering from a disturbance.}
