\caption[Maximum power transfer to a matched load]{The power delivered
to an external load $R$ from a source of electromotive force
$\mathcal{E}$ and internal resistance $R_{i}$, plotted against the load
ratio $R/R_{i}$. The curve rises from zero (a short circuit dissipates
no power in a vanishing load), reaches a single maximum, and falls
again (an open circuit carries no current). Setting $P'(R) = 0$ locates
the maximum at $R^{*} = R_{i}$: the load is matched to the source. The
peak power is $P^{*} = \mathcal{E}^{2}/(4R_{i})$, and the tangent is
horizontal there, the first-order stationary condition of the chapter.
The curve is broad near its top, so a moderate mismatch costs little
power, the same flat-optimum behaviour the inventory cost shows.}
