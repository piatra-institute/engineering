\caption[Catastrophic cancellation in the quadratic formula]{Correct
significant digits retained in the smaller root of $x^{2} + b x + c = 0$
as the two roots separate, measured by the ratio $b^{2}/(4c)$. When the
roots are far apart the small root is $x_{-} \approx -c/b$ and the naive
formula $(-b + \sqrt{b^{2} - 4c})/2$ subtracts two nearly equal
positive numbers, losing roughly one decimal digit for every decade of
separation. The algebraically equivalent form $x_{-} = 2c/(-b -
\sqrt{b^{2} - 4c})$ replaces the subtraction by an addition of
like-signed numbers and holds all sixteen digits. The well-conditioned
problem is destroyed by an unstable algorithm, the pattern section 16.2
names and the remedy it prescribes: rewrite before evaluating. Source:
authors' analysis following the catalogue of
\cite{text:goldberg1991-floating-point}.}
