\caption[The CFL condition as a domain-of-dependence constraint]{The
Courant-Friedrichs-Lewy condition read off the space-time stencil of the
explicit upwind scheme for the advection equation $u_{t} + a\,u_{x} = 0$.
The scheme updates $u_{i}^{n+1}$ from $u_{i}^{n}$ and $u_{i-1}^{n}$, so
its numerical domain of dependence is the interval $[x_{i-1}, x_{i}]$.
The true solution at $(x_{i}, t_{n+1})$ depends on the value carried
along the characteristic through that point, whose foot at time $t_{n}$
lies at $x_{i} - a\,\Delta t$. When the Courant number $\nu = a\,\Delta
t/\Delta x \leq 1$ (left) the foot stays inside the stencil and the
scheme can reconstruct the value; when $\nu > 1$ (right) the foot escapes
the stencil, the scheme has no access to the information that determines
the answer, and the error grows without bound. The condition is not a
round-off effect but a statement about information flow.
Source: authors' schematic after \cite{text:v2ch16-courant-friedrichs-lewy-1928}.}
