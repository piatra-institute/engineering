\caption[The merge-sort recursion tree]{Merge sort splits $n$ elements
into two halves, recurses, and merges in linear time. Each level of the
tree performs $n$ units of merge work in total, however the $n$ is
partitioned across the nodes of that level, and the tree has $\log_{2}
n$ levels down to the single-element leaves. The total work is $n\cdot
\log_{2} n$, which the induction in the text certifies for $n$ a power
of two. The asymptotic running time $O(n\log n)$ is read off the tree
and proved over all sizes by induction, a claim no finite benchmark
could establish.}
