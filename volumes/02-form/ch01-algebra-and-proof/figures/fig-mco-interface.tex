\caption[Mars Climate Orbiter interface failure]{The Mars Climate
Orbiter loss as an interface failure. Each side of the contractor-
to-operator boundary was internally consistent. The interface
specification correctly named newton seconds. The conversion factor
of $4.45$ between pound-force seconds and newton seconds never
appeared in any handoff, and no audit step at the interface enforced
the requirement. The per-burn bias was small. Over months of cruise
through angular-momentum-desaturation and trajectory-correction
manoeuvres, the bias accumulated into a trajectory deflection
sufficient to bring the spacecraft below atmospheric-survival
altitude at Mars arrival on 23 September 1999. The figure is drawn
from the Mishap Investigation Board's chain of events
\cite{acc:nasa-mco-mib}; the chapter's closing principle reads this
case as the canonical example of notation as interface contract.}
\label{fig:vol02:ch01:mco-interface}
