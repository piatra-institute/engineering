\caption[Three classes of function on finite sets]{Injective,
surjective, and bijective functions, drawn on small finite domains
and codomains. Injectivity is the property that distinguishes inputs
to distinguished outputs. Surjectivity is the property that every
output is hit. A bijection has both, and is the only kind of function
for which the inverse $f^{-1}$ exists as a function on the whole
codomain. The square function $f(x) = x^{2}$ on $\R$ is neither
injective (because $f(2) = f(-2)$) nor surjective (because negative
values are not hit); restricting its domain to $[0, \infty)$ makes
it bijective onto $[0, \infty)$ and produces the engineering square
root.}
