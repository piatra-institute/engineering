\caption[The SVD as rotation-scaling-rotation]{The singular value
decomposition factors every linear map into three operations performed
in sequence. The unit ball in $\R^{n}$ (a) is rotated by
$\mat{V}^{\transpose}$ to align with the matrix's preferred input
directions (b), scaled along each axis by the corresponding singular
value to produce an axis-aligned ellipsoid (c), and rotated by
$\mat{U}$ to align with the output basis (d). The semi-axes of the
output ellipse are $\sigma_{1}\vect{u}_{1}$ and
$\sigma_{2}\vect{u}_{2}$, exact for every matrix. A rank-deficient
matrix produces an ellipsoid that degenerates in some directions, a
nearly singular matrix produces an ellipsoid with one short axis, and
a well-conditioned matrix produces an ellipsoid with axes of
comparable length. The condition number $\kappa = \sigma_{1}/\sigma_{r}$
is the aspect ratio of the output ellipsoid.}
\label{fig:vol02:ch10:svd-geometry}
\end{figure}
