\caption[Machine-state occupancy converging to stationarity]{The
occupancy probabilities of the five-state machine-reliability chain
of \cref{sec:vol02:ch10:markov-casestudy}, started from the
all-running state, converging to the stationary distribution. The
fast initial transient (the first half-dozen shifts) is governed by
the smaller subdominant eigenvalues; the slow approach of the last
fraction of a percent is governed by the second-largest eigenvalue
magnitude $\lvert\lambda_{2}\rvert \approx 0.58$, which sets the
mixing time near $1/(1 - 0.58) \approx 2.4$ shifts. The stationary
occupancy of the unplanned-down state, $\pi_{\text{down}} \approx
0.031$, together with the degraded and maintenance occupancies, is
what the case study turns into a long-run availability and an annual
downtime-cost figure. After roughly ten shifts the occupancy is a
property of the transition structure alone, not of where the machine
started.}
