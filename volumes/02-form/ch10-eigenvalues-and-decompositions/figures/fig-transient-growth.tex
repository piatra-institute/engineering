\caption[Transient growth from a stable spectrum]{The response norm
$\lVert e^{\mat{A}t}\vect{x}_{0}\rVert_{2}$ for the non-normal matrix
of \cref{sec:vol02:ch10:failure} (red), whose eigenvalues are
$-0.5$ and $-0.6$, both with negative real part. The asymptotic decay
is guaranteed by the spectrum, and for large $t$ the norm does fall to
zero. On the way down it first climbs to roughly seven times its
initial value near $t \approx 2$, a transient amplification driven
entirely by the non-orthogonality of the two nearly parallel
eigenvectors. A normal matrix with the same eigenvalues (blue dashed)
decays monotonically from the start. The eigenvalues answer the
asymptotic question correctly and the transient question not at all;
a saturation limit, a stress allowable, or a linearisation validity
bound crossed near the peak turns a spectrally stable design into a
deployed failure.}
\label{fig:vol02:ch10:transient-growth}
\end{figure}
