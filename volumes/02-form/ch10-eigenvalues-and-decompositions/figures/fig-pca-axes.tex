\caption[Principal component axes overlaid on a 2D scatter]{Principal
component analysis on a small two-feature dataset. The blue points are
the samples; the red dot is the centroid $\bar{\vect{x}}$. The two
principal axes (red, longer; amber, shorter) point along the
eigenvectors of the sample covariance matrix, with lengths proportional
to the standard deviations $\sqrt{\sigma_{i}^{2}/(m-1)}$ along each
direction. The data spread along $\vect{v}_{1}$ is several times the
spread along $\vect{v}_{2}$, so projecting to one dimension along
$\vect{v}_{1}$ retains most of the variance and a downstream model
that only sees that projection still resolves the samples. Centring
the data is a structural prerequisite; otherwise the first principal
direction would point toward the centroid rather than along the
spread.}
\label{fig:vol02:ch10:pca-axes}
\end{figure}
