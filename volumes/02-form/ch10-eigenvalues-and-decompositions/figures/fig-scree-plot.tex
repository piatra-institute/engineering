\caption[A scree plot and its two readings]{The scree plot is the
diagnostic that closes every spectral-method analysis in this
chapter. The bars (left axis) give the variance carried by each
component, $\sigma_{k}^{2}$, ordered from largest to smallest; the
shape is a steep face followed by a shallow talus, and the index
where the curve bends is the elbow. Choosing the retained rank $k$ at
the elbow (here $k \approx 3$) keeps the structural components and
discards the noise. The red curve (right axis) is the cumulative
explained-variance ratio, the running sum of variances normalised by
the total; reading it against a horizontal retention line (here
$90\%$) gives a second candidate $k$. The two readings rarely agree
exactly, and the chapter's discipline is to report both and let the
downstream task break the tie. A scree plot with no clear elbow warns
that no honest low-rank truncation exists.}
\label{fig:vol02:ch10:scree-plot}
