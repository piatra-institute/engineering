\caption[A non-convex Pareto front and the weighted-sum blind
spot]{A Pareto front with a re-entrant (non-convex) region, both
objectives minimised so the front is the lower-left boundary of the
achievable set. Weighted-sum scalarisation minimises $w_{1} f_{1} +
w_{2} f_{2}$, whose contours are straight lines; sweeping the weights
sweeps the slope of the contour, and the contour can only come to rest
tangent to the convex hull of the front. Points in the dimpled
interior (red) are Pareto optimal yet no weight vector reaches them,
so a weighted-sum sweep silently under-reports the achievable designs.
The $\epsilon$-constraint method minimises one objective subject to an
upper bound $\epsilon$ on the other; the vertical bound slices the
front and recovers the dimpled point, and sweeping $\epsilon$ recovers
the whole front, convex or not, at the cost of a constrained solve at
each sweep value.}
