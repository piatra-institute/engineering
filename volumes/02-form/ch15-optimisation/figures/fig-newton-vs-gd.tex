\caption[Newton's method against gradient descent on a
quadratic]{Newton's method and gradient descent on the same
anisotropic quadratic, started from the same point $\vect{x}_{0}$.
Newton multiplies the gradient by the inverse Hessian, which rescales
the two coordinate directions by their curvatures and points the step
straight at the minimum: for any strictly convex quadratic the single
Newton step is exact, independent of the condition number. Gradient
descent at its best symmetric step $\eta^{*} = 2/(\mu + L)$ contracts
by the factor $(\kappa - 1)/(\kappa + 1) = 3/5$ per step and
alternates sign across the steep axis, so it spirals in over many
steps. Newton buys the one-step finish with an $O(n^{3})$ Hessian
solve; gradient descent pays $O(n)$ per step but many more steps, and
the crossover between them is the method-selection judgment of the
chapter.}
