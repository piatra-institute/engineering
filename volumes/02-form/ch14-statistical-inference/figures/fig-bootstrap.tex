\caption[The bootstrap as a plug-in approximation to the sampling
distribution]{The bootstrap procedure of \textcite{paper:v2ch14-efron-bootstrap-1979}.
The original sample is treated as a stand-in for the population.
$B$ resamples of size $n$ are drawn from it with replacement; on
each resample the statistic $\hat{\theta}$ is recomputed; the
resulting $B$-element collection $\{\hat{\theta}^{(b)}\}$ is the
empirical bootstrap distribution. Its variance estimates the
sampling variance of $\hat{\theta}$; its quantiles estimate a
confidence interval (the percentile method). The bootstrap is the
plug-in principle applied to the sampling distribution itself: when
no closed-form standard error exists, the empirical distribution
substitutes for the unknown population and the resampling generates
the variability. The procedure works under regularity, fails for
statistics whose functional form is sensitive to extreme values
(maxima, minima, sample quantiles near the boundary), and is the
engineer's default when an asymptotic formula is unavailable
\cite{paper:ch08-efron-tibshirani-1993}.}
