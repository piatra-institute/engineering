\caption{The Chinese remainder theorem as a bijection. With coprime
moduli $3$ and $5$ each of the $15$ residues modulo $15$ lands in a
distinct cell of the $3\times 5$ grid indexed by its residue pair
(mod $3$, mod $5$), and every cell is filled exactly once. Solving a
system such as $x\equiv 2\pmod 3$, $x\equiv 3\pmod 5$ is reading off
the single cell $(2,3)$, here holding $8$; the same construction with
moduli $3,5,7$ places Sun-tzu's number at $23\pmod{105}$. The grid
is why a computation modulo a large composite splits into independent
computations modulo its coprime factors and recombines without loss.}
