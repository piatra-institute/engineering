\caption[Copula tail dependence]{Two dependence structures with the
same correlation in the bulk but opposite joint-tail behaviour, drawn
on the unit square of a copula. Left, the Gaussian copula: the cloud
thins toward the corners (dashed boxes), so the probability that both
variables are jointly extreme decays faster than either margin, the
asymptotic tail independence of section 13.5. Right, a $t$-copula:
extra mass fills the lower-left and upper-right corners, so extremes
arrive together, the positive tail dependence the Gaussian copula
erases. Identical margins and a matched central correlation can hide
this difference entirely, which is why the Gaussian-copula default
model understated joint default in the 2008 case of section 13.9. The
points illustrate the corner mass and are not simulated draws. Source:
schematic by the authors.}
\label{fig:vol02ch13:copula-tails}
