\caption[The law of total variance]{The variance decomposition
$\mathrm{Var}(Y)=\mathrm{E}[\mathrm{Var}(Y\mid X)]+\mathrm{Var}(\mathrm{E}[Y\mid X])$ read off
three production lines. Each line has its own conditional mean
$\mathrm{E}[Y\mid X=i]$ (filled squares at $3$, $6$, $9$) and its own
within-line scatter (open circles). The total variance of $Y$ splits into
a between-group part, the variance of the conditional means about the
grand mean (the vertical span of the squares, the explained variance), and
a within-group part, the average scatter inside a line (the unexplained,
residual variance). Conditioning on the line removes the between-group
part and leaves only the within-line scatter, which is why knowing the
line sharpens a prediction of $Y$ by exactly the explained fraction. This
decomposition is the probabilistic skeleton of the analysis of variance
and of variance-component models in metrology. Source: illustrative data
generated by the authors.}
