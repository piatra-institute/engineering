  \fill[engblue!22] (0,0) rectangle (5.4,3.6);
  \draw[engblue, very thick] (0,0) rectangle (5.4,3.6);

\caption[Microstrip patch antenna plan view]{Plan view of the rectangular
microstrip patch of the third case study. The patch of width $W$ and
resonant length $L$ sits on a grounded dielectric substrate of relative
permittivity $\varepsilon_r$ and thickness $h$. The two edges separated by
$L$ are the radiating edges; the field fringes from each into free space and
the pair acts as a two-element array broadside to the board. An inset
microstrip feed of depth $y_0$ taps the patch where the standing-wave
resistance has fallen from its high edge value to the $50\,\si{\ohm}$ of the
feed line, which matches the antenna without a separate transformer.}
