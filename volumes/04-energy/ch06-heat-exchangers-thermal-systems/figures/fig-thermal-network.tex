\caption[Thermal-resistance network across a heat-exchanger wall]{The overall
heat-transfer coefficient as a series of thermal resistances from the hot fluid
to the cold fluid. Heat crosses the inside convective film, the inside fouling
layer, the tube-wall conduction, the outside fouling layer, and the outside
convective film, and the resistances add in series exactly as electrical
resistances do. The product $UA$ is the reciprocal of the total resistance, so
the largest single resistance sets the floor on performance; adding area or
turbulence on the side that is already low-resistance buys little. Fouling
appears as two extra resistances in the chain, which is why a thin deposit on the
controlling side can dominate the sum and halve the duty.}
\label{fig:vol04ch06:thermal-network}
