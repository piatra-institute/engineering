\caption[One-shell-pass, two-tube-pass shell-and-tube exchanger]{Schematic of
a one-shell-pass, two-tube-pass shell-and-tube exchanger. The hot fluid enters
the channel head, passes left to right through the upper tube pass, turns at the
floating head, and returns through the lower pass to the outlet, so the tube
side makes two passes for one shell pass. The cold fluid enters the shell and is
forced to weave across the bundle by the segmental baffles, which alternate their
open cut between top and bottom to set a cross-flow component and to support the
tubes against flow-induced vibration. Because the tube fluid runs with the shell
fluid on one pass and against it on the other, the arrangement is neither pure
counter-flow nor pure parallel-flow, and a correction factor $F<1$ applies to
the counter-flow log-mean temperature difference.}
