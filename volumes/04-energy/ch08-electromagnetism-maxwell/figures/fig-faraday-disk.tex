% radius a spins at angular rate omega in an axial field B. Each radial
% element sweeps flux, so a steady EMF appears between the axle and the rim,
% E = (1/2) B omega a^2, with no commutator and no reversal.

\caption[The Faraday disk]{The Faraday disk, or homopolar generator: a
conducting disk of radius $a$ spun at angular rate $\omega$ in an axial field
$\vect{B}$. Each free charge at radius $r$ moves with the disk at speed
$\omega r$ and feels a radial magnetic force $q\omega r B$, which drives
positive charge outward until an electrostatic field balances it. Integrating
that force per unit charge from the axle to the rim gives a steady EMF
$\mathcal{E}=\int_0^a \omega r B\,\dd r = \tfrac12 B\omega a^{2}$, tapped
between a brush on the axle and a brush on the rim. The output is genuinely
direct: it does not reverse and needs no commutator, because every radial
element sweeps flux in the same sense throughout the rotation. The device is
the cleanest generator there is and the reason it puzzled early workers, who
could not locate the moving flux in a circuit whose geometry never changes.}
\label{fig:vol04ch08:faraday-disk}
\end{figure}
