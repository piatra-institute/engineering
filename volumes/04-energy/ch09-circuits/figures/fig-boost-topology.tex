\caption[Boost converter power stage]{The power stage of a boost (step-up)
converter. When the switch closes it grounds the switch node, so the full input
voltage stands across the inductor and its current ramps up, storing energy in
the magnetic field while the diode is reverse-biased and the output capacitor
alone supplies the load. When the switch opens, the inductor current forces the
switch node above the output, the diode conducts, and the inductor dumps its
stored energy into the capacitor and the load at a voltage higher than the
input. Volt-second balance across the inductor sets the ratio: the input drives
the inductor for the on-time and the input-minus-output drives it for the
off-time, so the output rises above the input by a factor set by the duty cycle,
the step-up the topology is named for.}
