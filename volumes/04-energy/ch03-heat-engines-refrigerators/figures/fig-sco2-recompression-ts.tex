% Figure: the supercritical CO2 recompression Brayton cycle on a schematic T-s
% plane. A single main compression near the critical point, a split of the flow
% into a recompressor branch that bypasses the precooler, two recuperators (low
% and high temperature), heat addition, and turbine expansion. Drawn as a closed
% loop of straight segments with the recompression branch shown rejoining before
% the high-temperature recuperator. The point is the reduced heat rejection and
% the recuperator duty, not exact property values.

\begin{tikzpicture}
\begin{axis}[

\addplot[engblue, very thick] coordinates {(2,32) (2.4,90)};
\addlegendentry{main compressor}
% Low-T recuperator cold side: 2 -> 2b heating 90 -> 250 at rising s
\addplot[engamber, very thick] coordinates {(2.4,90) (3.4,250)};
\addlegendentry{recuperation (cold side)}
% recompressor branch: from precooler inlet region, state 1b (150,s=3.0) compress to (250,s=3.4) rejoining
\addplot[engred, very thick, dashed] coordinates {(3.0,150) (3.4,250)};
\addlegendentry{recompressor branch}

\addplot[engamber, very thick] coordinates {(3.4,250) (4.6,430)};

\addplot[engorange, very thick] coordinates {(4.6,430) (6.2,600)};

\addplot[engblue, very thick] coordinates {(6.2,600) (8.4,470)};

\addplot[engamber, very thick] coordinates {(8.4,470) (7.2,270)};

\addplot[engamber, very thick] coordinates {(7.2,270) (6.2,150)};

\caption[Supercritical CO$_2$ recompression Brayton cycle on the temperature-entropy
plane]{A schematic temperature-entropy path of the supercritical carbon-dioxide
recompression Brayton cycle. The working fluid is compressed near its critical
point, where its density is high and the compression work is small, then heated
through two recuperators that recover turbine-exhaust heat before the primary
heater lifts it to the turbine inlet temperature. A fraction of the flow is
diverted to a recompressor that bypasses the precooler and rejoins upstream of the
high-temperature recuperator; this split is what lets the recuperators absorb the
mismatch in heat capacity between the high-pressure and low-pressure streams that
the strong property variation near the critical point creates. The cycle rejects
only the heat the recuperators cannot recover, in the precooler, and its
compactness and high efficiency at turbine inlet temperatures of $500$ to
$700\,^{\circ}$C make it a candidate for nuclear, concentrated-solar, and
waste-heat power where the steam cycle's size and water demand are liabilities.}
\label{fig:vol04ch03:sco2-recompression-ts}
\end{figure}
