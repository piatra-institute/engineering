% diffusion length L; a shorter lifetime steepens the fall. Sample points from

\caption[Minority-carrier diffusion profile]{Excess minority-carrier density in
the quasi-neutral region of a forward-biased junction, normalised to its value at
the depletion edge. The injected excess decays exponentially into the bulk with
the diffusion length $L=\sqrt{D\tau}$, the distance a minority carrier diffuses
before it recombines, and it falls to $1/e$ of its edge value at exactly one
diffusion length. A shorter carrier lifetime (dashed) steepens the fall and
shortens $L$, which raises the saturation current and lowers a solar cell's
collection. The diffusion current that sets the Shockley saturation current is
the slope of this profile at the edge, $eD\,\Delta n(0)/L$, so the diffusion
length ties the material's carrier lifetime directly to the diode's reverse
current.}
\label{fig:vol04ch12:diffusion-profile}
\end{figure}
