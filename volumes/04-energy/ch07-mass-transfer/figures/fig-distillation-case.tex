\caption[Worked McCabe-Thiele staircase for the benzene-toluene
column]{The McCabe-Thiele construction carried for the benzene-toluene
worked example. The equilibrium curve uses a constant relative volatility
$\alpha=2.4$. The rectifying line of slope $R/(R+1)=0.643$ runs from the
distillate corner $(0.95,0.95)$ to the feed pinch; the stripping line of
slope $1.445$ runs from the bottoms corner $(0.05,0.05)$ to the same
pinch, and the saturated-liquid feed gives a vertical q-line at
$z_F=0.45$. Stepping the staircase from the distillate corner counts the
equilibrium stages and locates the feed stage where the staircase passes
the pinch, near the seventh step from the top. The staircase reaches the
bottoms specification $x_W=0.05$ at about the fourteenth stage, the count
the text carries arithmetically.}
