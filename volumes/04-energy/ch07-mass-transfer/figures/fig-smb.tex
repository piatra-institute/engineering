% Figure: the four-zone simulated-moving-bed (SMB) schematic. Several packed
% columns are joined in a loop; the four external ports (feed, desorbent,
% extract, raffinate) are advanced one column downstream at fixed intervals,
% which mimics counter-current motion of the solid against the fluid without
% actually moving the packing. The four zones between the ports each do one
% job: zone I regenerates the sorbent, zone II purifies the extract, zone III
% purifies the raffinate, and zone IV cleans the recycle fluid.

\caption[Four-zone simulated-moving-bed schematic]{A simulated moving bed
joins several fixed packed columns in a closed loop and advances the four
external ports, desorbent in, extract out, feed in, raffinate out, one column
downstream at each switching interval. The stepping of the ports against a
circulating fluid imitates a solid moving counter-currently to the fluid,
which a truly moving bed cannot do without attrition. The four zones between
consecutive ports each carry one duty: regenerating the sorbent, purifying the
strongly held component into the extract, purifying the weakly held component
into the raffinate, and cleaning the fluid before it recycles. Continuous
counter-current contact is what lets the unit separate a binary at high purity
and high yield with far less sorbent and desorbent than a batch column.}
\label{fig:vol04ch07:smb}
\end{figure}
