% Figure: the planetary entropy budget drawn as an energy-in / entropy-out
% ledger. The Earth absorbs and re-emits nearly the same power in steady state,
% about 1.2e17 W, but the entropy carried by that power differs enormously
% because the incoming sunlight arrives at ~5800 K and the outgoing infrared
% leaves at ~255 K. Entropy flow = (4/3) x power / temperature for radiation.
% The bar chart contrasts the small incoming entropy flux against the large
% outgoing one; the difference is the entropy the planet exports, the budget the
% climate system and the biosphere run on.
\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\begin{tikzpicture}

  xmin=0, xmax=8,
  ytick={1,2,3},
  yticklabels={{absorbed sunlight\\(in, $\sim 5800$ K)}, {emitted infrared\\(out, $\sim 255$ K)}, {net exported}},
  yticklabel style={font=\scriptsize, align=right},
  tick label style={font=\scriptsize},
  label style={font=\small},
  xmajorgrids=true,
  grid style={enggray!22},
  nodes near coords,

% incoming: (4/3)*1.2e17/5800 = 2.76e13 -> 0.28 in units of 1e14
\addplot[fill=engblue!70, draw=engblue] coordinates {(0.28,1)};
% outgoing: (4/3)*1.2e17/255 = 6.27e14 -> 6.27
\addplot[fill=engred!65, draw=engred] coordinates {(6.27,2)};
% net exported = difference
\addplot[fill=englime!60!black, draw=englime!40!black] coordinates {(5.99,3)};
\end{axis}
\end{tikzpicture}
\caption[The planetary entropy budget]{The Earth's steady-state entropy ledger.
The planet absorbs and re-radiates nearly the same power, of order
$1.2\times10^{17}\,\text{W}$, so a first-law energy balance shows almost nothing:
energy in equals energy out. The entropy ledger tells the story. Radiation
carries entropy at the rate $\tfrac43$ of its power divided by its temperature,
and the incoming sunlight arrives from a $5800\,\si{\kelvin}$ photosphere while
the outgoing infrared leaves at a $255\,\si{\kelvin}$ effective sky temperature.
The Earth therefore takes in a small entropy flux and sheds a large one, and the
difference, the net exported entropy, is the low-entropy resource that drives
photosynthesis, weather, and ocean circulation. The planet is a heat engine
running between the Sun and deep space, and the work it does on its own fluids
and its biosphere is paid for entirely by this entropy export, not by any net
energy gain.}
\label{fig:vol04ch04:earth-entropy-budget}
\end{figure}
