% Figure: the Landauer cost of erasure against the real energy budget of storage.
% The thermodynamic floor for erasing one bit is k_B T ln 2, about 3e-21 J at room
% temperature. Real storage and logic dissipate many orders of magnitude more per
% bit. We draw a logarithmic bar chart of energy-per-bit across the Landauer floor
% and a few representative real technologies, to make the size of the headroom
% visible. The values are order-of-magnitude figures for orientation, not a
% benchmark of any particular device. We use numeric y positions with manual
% tick labels to avoid the whitespace pitfall of symbolic coordinates.
\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\begin{tikzpicture}
\begin{axis}[
  width=12cm, height=7cm,
  xbar,
  xmode=log,
  xmin=1e-21, xmax=1e-6,
  ymin=0.3, ymax=5.7,
  ytick={1,2,3,4,5},
  yticklabels={Landauer floor, research adiabatic, low-power CMOS bit op,
    DRAM bit write, disk-drive bit erase},
  xlabel={energy dissipated per bit (J), logarithmic},
  tick label style={font=\scriptsize},
  label style={font=\small},
  bar width=10pt,
  nodes near coords={\pgfmathprintnumber[sci, sci zerofill, precision=0]{\pgfplotspointmeta}},
  nodes near coords style={font=\scriptsize, anchor=west},
  point meta=x,
]
\addplot[draw=engblue, fill=engblue!25] coordinates {
  (2.9e-21, 1)
  (1e-18,   2)
  (1e-15,   3)
  (1e-12,   4)
  (1e-9,    5)
};
\end{axis}
\end{tikzpicture}
\caption[The Landauer floor against the energy budget of real storage]{The
Landauer floor $k_B T \ln 2 \approx 2.9\times10^{-21}\,\text{J}$ for erasing one
bit at room temperature, set against order-of-magnitude figures for the energy
real technologies spend per bit. The horizontal axis is logarithmic and spans
twelve decades, the measure of how far practical computation runs above its
thermodynamic floor. The floor is the heat any logically irreversible operation
must release: erasing a bit collapses two prior microstates into one, a
two-to-one loss of phase-space volume that carries an entropy decrease $k_B \ln 2$
in the memory and so must export at least $k_B T \ln 2$ of heat to the
surroundings. Real devices dissipate the floor many times over because switching
charge, driving wires, and overcoming leakage cost far more than the information
erasure itself. The gap is the headroom that reversible and adiabatic logic chase;
the floor itself is fixed by the second law and falls only with temperature.}
\label{fig:vol04ch04:landauer-erasure}
\end{figure}
