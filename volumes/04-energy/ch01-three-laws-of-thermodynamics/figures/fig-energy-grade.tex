% Figure: the quality (grade) of energy. Two bars carry the same quantity of
% energy (100 units) but different exergy content: high-grade electrical work is
% nearly all exergy, while low-grade heat delivered near ambient is nearly all
% anergy (unavailable). A resistance heater degrades the first into the second.
\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\begin{tikzpicture}[font=\small]
  % baseline
  \draw[enggray] (0,0) -- (9.5,0);
  % === bar 1: high-grade electrical work ===
  \filldraw[englime!75!engblack] (0.6,0) rectangle (2.2,4.6);
  \filldraw[enggray!30] (0.6,4.6) rectangle (2.2,4.9);
  \node[font=\scriptsize, anchor=north, align=center] at (1.4,-0.15)
    {electrical\\work};
  \node[white, font=\scriptsize, align=center] at (1.4,2.3) {exergy\\$\approx 100$};
  % === bar 2: heat at high temperature (flame) ===
  \filldraw[englime!75!engblack] (3.4,0) rectangle (5.0,3.4);
  \filldraw[enggray!30] (3.4,3.4) rectangle (5.0,4.9);
  \node[font=\scriptsize, anchor=north, align=center] at (4.2,-0.15)
    {heat at\\$1500\,\mathrm{K}$};
  \node[white, font=\scriptsize, align=center] at (4.2,1.7) {exergy\\$\approx 80$};
  \node[enggray, font=\scriptsize] at (4.2,4.15) {anergy};

\caption[The grade of energy: exergy versus anergy]{Three carriers of the same
hundred units of energy, split into the exergy (the part convertible to work
against a $T_0 \approx 300\,\mathrm{K}$ environment) and the anergy (the part
that is not). Electrical work is almost pure exergy; heat delivered at a flame
temperature carries most of its energy as exergy; heat delivered a few kelvin
above ambient, such as a condenser reject stream, is almost pure anergy and can
do little work. The first law counts all three bars equal at a hundred units. A
resistance heater takes the first bar and produces the third, conserving energy
while destroying nearly all the exergy, which is the quantitative content of the
claim that resistance heating is the most wasteful use of electricity: it is a
first-law identity and a second-law catastrophe at once.}
\label{fig:vol04ch01:energy-grade}
\end{figure}
