\caption[The flow-work construction behind enthalpy]{The flow-work
construction that turns internal energy into enthalpy for any stream
crossing a boundary. To push a slug of mass $\delta m$ across the inlet,
the upstream fluid does work $p_1 v_1\,\delta m$ on the control volume; to
expel a slug across the outlet, the control volume does work
$p_2 v_2\,\delta m$ on the downstream fluid. Each slug therefore carries,
beyond its internal energy $u$, the flow work $pv$ needed to move it across
the boundary, and the two combine into the enthalpy $h = u + pv$. The shaft
work $\dot{W}_s$ and heat $\dot{Q}$ cross the dashed boundary separately.
The grouping is why every open-system balance is written in enthalpy rather
than internal energy: the flow work is already inside $h$.}
\label{fig:vol04ch01:flow-work}
\end{figure}
