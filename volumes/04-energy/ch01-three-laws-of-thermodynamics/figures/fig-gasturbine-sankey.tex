\caption[Energy flow through a simple-cycle gas turbine]{Energy flow through a
simple-cycle industrial gas turbine, drawn to scale for the case study of this
section. The fuel energy (the lower heating value of the natural gas) enters at
the left. A small part is lost to incomplete combustion, casing radiation, and
bearing friction; the rest is released into the gas path. The turbine drives
the compressor and delivers the remainder as net shaft work, while the largest
single stream leaves as the enthalpy of the hot exhaust. The contrast with the
steam-plant diagram is the location of the big rejection: a gas turbine throws
its waste heat out of a hot stack rather than into cooling water, which is why
the exhaust is worth recovering. A combined-cycle plant feeds this exhaust into
a steam boiler and lifts the overall efficiency into the high fifties.}
\label{fig:vol04ch01:gasturbine-sankey}
