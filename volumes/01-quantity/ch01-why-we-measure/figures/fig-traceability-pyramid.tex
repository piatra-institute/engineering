\caption[Traceability pyramid: from BIPM to the working bench]{The
traceability pyramid for a working measurement. At the apex sits the
Bureau International des Poids et Mesures (BIPM) in S\`evres, France
\cite{hist:ch01t3-metre-convention-1875}, and the National Metrology
Institutes that realise the SI base units against the defining
constants: NIST in the United States, the National Physical Laboratory
(NPL) in the United Kingdom \cite{web:ch01t3-npl-primary}, the
Physikalisch-Technische Bundesanstalt (PTB) in Germany, and the
National Institute of Metrology (NIM) in China
\cite{web:ch01t3-nim}. The middle tier holds accredited calibration
laboratories operating under ISO/IEC 17025
\cite{std:ch01t3-iso-iec-17025}; in the United States, accreditation
is administered by A2LA \cite{web:ch01t3-a2la}, in the United Kingdom
by UKAS \cite{web:ch01t3-ukas}. The base tier is the working
laboratory at the bench, in the plant, on the line, in the home. The
arrows carry calibration certificates and uncertainty budgets; a
working measurement at the base whose chain to the apex is intact is
called \emph{traceable}, and the chain is the subject of Chapter 3.}
\label{fig:vol01:ch01:traceability-pyramid}
