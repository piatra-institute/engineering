\caption[Target diagram: precision and accuracy]{The four cases of
precision and accuracy on a bullseye target. Precision is the
closeness of the shots to one another; accuracy is the closeness of
the shots' centre to the bullseye. The two are independent: a
precise pattern may sit far from the bullseye (top right; correctable
by calibration); a wide pattern may centre on the bullseye (bottom
left; correctable by averaging). The working laboratory standard
(top left) is both; the unmaintained instrument (bottom right) is
neither.}
