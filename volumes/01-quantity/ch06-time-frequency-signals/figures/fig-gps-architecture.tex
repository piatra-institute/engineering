\caption[GPS as a measurement system]{The GPS measurement chain.
Each satellite (SV) broadcasts a signal disciplined by its on-board
atomic clocks, steered by the master control station against GPS
system time, which is itself steered to UTC(USNO). The receiver
records the time of arrival against its local oscillator and forms
a pseudorange to each satellite. Four satellites in view supply
four pseudoranges, which solve four unknowns: the receiver
coordinates $(x, y, z)$ and the receiver clock offset $\Delta
t_{\text{rx}}$. Each block in the chain contributes its own error
budget; the working conversion $1 \,\text{ns} \Leftrightarrow 30
\,\text{cm}$ on the pseudorange anchors a reader's intuition.
Relativistic corrections are pre-applied at the satellite
frequency standard and refined in the receiver firmware. Position
service quoted current as of 2026; receiver and broadcast
performance figures vary with configuration, environment, and
processing.}
