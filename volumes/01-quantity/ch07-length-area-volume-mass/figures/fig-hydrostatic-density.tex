\caption[Density by hydrostatic weighing]{Hydrostatic weighing reads
volume as a mass difference rather than as a graduated reading. The
sample is weighed in air $(m_{\mathrm{air}})$ and again while fully
submerged $(m_{\mathrm{sub}})$; the apparent loss of weight equals the
weight of the displaced water, so the sample volume is
$V = (m_{\mathrm{air}} - m_{\mathrm{sub}})/\rho_{w}$ and the density
follows without any volumetric graduation. The method inherits the
balance's precision, which is why it reaches volume uncertainty an
order of magnitude below a graduated cylinder on the same object. The
temperature of the water sets $\rho_{w}$ and is the first quantity to
record. Source: editors' schematic.}
