\caption[Three volume methods discriminate porosity structure]{Three
independent volume methods on a single sintered-alumina specimen
report three different volumes, and the ordered disagreement is the
information. Water displacement reads the bulk (envelope) volume,
which counts open and closed pores as part of the solid; helium
pycnometry reads the skeletal volume, excluding the open pores helium
can enter; industrial CT segmentation counts the closed internal
pores as solid but excludes the surface-connected open pores. The gap
between displacement and pycnometry is the open-pore fraction; the gap
between pycnometry and CT is the closed-pore fraction. Convergence on
a single number would have hidden the porosity structure entirely.
Source: editors' worked figures for the sinter-cycle check in the
text.}
