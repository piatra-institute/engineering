\caption[Gamma-Poisson conjugate update of a failure rate]{The
gamma-Poisson conjugate update for a reliability rate estimate. The
prior $\text{Gamma}(\alpha = 2, \beta = 20)$ encodes a mean rate
$\alpha/\beta = 0.10$ failures per $10^{3}\,$h with wide spread. A
field campaign observes $K = 6$ failures over $T = 40$
thousand-hour units of exposure; the posterior is
$\text{Gamma}(\alpha + K, \beta + T) = \text{Gamma}(8, 60)$, mean
$8/60 \approx 0.133$, tighter than the prior. The update is
arithmetic: add observed failures to the first parameter and
observed exposure to the second. The vertical axis scales the two
densities to a common display height; the figure shows the shift
and the sharpening, not the absolute density.}
\label{fig:vol01:ch08:gamma-poisson-update}
