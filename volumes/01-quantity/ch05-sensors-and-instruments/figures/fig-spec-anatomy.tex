\caption[Anatomy of a sensor specification]{The terms a datasheet
defines, made visible on a single transfer plot. Offset is the
output at zero input; nonlinearity is the deviation of the actual
curve from the best-fit straight line; hysteresis is the gap
between rising and falling traces; resolution is the step size of
the smallest discernible change; range bounds the calibrated
region; saturation begins where the sensor stops following the
input; drift is the slow migration of the whole curve over time.
Bandwidth and response time live in a time-domain plot, not here.
Accuracy is the closeness of the actual curve to the ideal;
precision is the closeness of repeated readings to one another for
the same input. A reader who confuses precision with accuracy
will buy the wrong sensor.}
