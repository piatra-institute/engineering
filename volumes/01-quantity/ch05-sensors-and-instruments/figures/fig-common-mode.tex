\caption[Common-mode failure: independent vs shared cause]{Three
sensors voting on a measurement. Independent failures (left)
respond to the cause the vote was designed to handle: a single
sensor disagreeing with the other two is overruled by the
majority. A shared cause (right), ice crystals around a pitot
suite, the same firmware build in three inertial reference units,
or three voltage transducers from the same batch calibrated against
the same drifting secondary reference, defeats the vote. The
bottom panel shows the standard uncertainty of the mean of three
sensors with pairwise correlation $\rho$ between their errors,
relative to a single sensor's standard deviation $\sigma$. At
$\rho = 0$ the mean is $1/\sqrt{3} \approx 0.58$ times the
single-sensor spread; at $\rho \to 1$ the ratio approaches one and
the redundancy delivers no precision benefit.}
\label{fig:vol01:ch05:common-mode}
