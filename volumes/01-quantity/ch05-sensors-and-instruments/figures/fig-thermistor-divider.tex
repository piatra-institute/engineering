\caption[Thermistor voltage divider: schematic and transfer]{Left:
the chapter project's voltage divider with the fixed reference
resistor as the upper element and the NTC thermistor as the lower
element. The ADC tap reads
$V_{\text{out}} = V_{\text{cc}} R_{\text{th}}/(R_{\text{ref}} +
R_{\text{th}})$. Right: the resulting transfer curve for a
$10\,\text{k}\Omega$ NTC thermistor with $\beta = 3950\,\text{K}$
across the kitchen-temperature range. The slope $|\dd V_{\text{out}}
/ \dd T|$ is largest in the middle of the range (shaded), where
$R_{\text{th}}$ is close to $R_{\text{ref}}$; the divider's
small-signal sensitivity is approximately
$\Delta V_{\text{out}}/V_{\text{out}} \approx \varepsilon/2$ for a
fractional resistance change $\varepsilon$ at the symmetric
operating point.}
