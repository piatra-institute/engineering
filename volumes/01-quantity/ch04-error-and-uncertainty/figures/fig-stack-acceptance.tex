\caption[Tolerance-stack acceptance fraction]{Assembly-length
distribution for the three-part stack of \S 4.3.7 with one part's
tolerance widened to $\pm 0.08\,\si{\milli\meter}$ and skewed (a
lognormal manufacturing distribution truncated at the upper spec),
the other two left as centred Gaussians at $\pm 0.05\,\si{\milli
\meter}$. The linear root-sum-square Gaussian (solid blue) predicts
acceptance fraction 99.4\% inside the assembly specification limits
$\pm 0.10\,\si{\milli\meter}$, the symmetric prediction the textbook
formula gives. The Monte Carlo result (amber histogram, $10^6$
draws) predicts acceptance fraction 96.6\%, with the upper-tail
out-of-spec rate of 3.4\% driving the gap. The linear formula
underestimates rejection by a factor of $\sim 6$ here because the
asymmetric input distribution leaks more probability into one tail
than the symmetric Gaussian model admits. Reference implementation
in \texttt{code/tolerance\_stack\_acceptance.py}.}
