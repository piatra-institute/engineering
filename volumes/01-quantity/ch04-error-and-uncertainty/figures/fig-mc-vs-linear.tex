\caption[Monte Carlo vs linear propagation for pendulum $g$]{Empirical
distribution of $g = 4\pi^{2} L/T^{2}$ from $10^{5}$ Monte Carlo
samples (blue) with Gaussian inputs, compared to the Gaussian
prediction from linear propagation (red curve). The two agree
because the input relative uncertainties are small (below $0.5\%$).
For models with larger relative input uncertainties the empirical
distribution becomes asymmetric and the linear prediction breaks
down.}
