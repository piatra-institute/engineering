# Interludes between volumes

Interludes are short bridging passages between the twelve volumes, settled in question 14 of `open-questions.md`:

> There should be short interludes between volumes. They bridge the intellectual arc, summarize what has changed in the reader's model of the world, and prepare the next domain without carrying heavy technical content.

Eleven interludes sit between the twelve volumes, written inline in `main.tex` as `\begin{interlude}...\end{interlude}` blocks. They are the only places lyric prose is permitted (settled question 24).

This file collects the current text of each interlude, planned revisions, and open editorial notes. Revisions are not applied to `main.tex` until the entry below is marked **Apply**.

## I → II  (Quantity into Form)

**Current.** Closes Volume I; opens Volume II by naming the move from numbers to a language of change and structure.

**Status.** Stable. No revision queued.

## II → III  (Form into Force)

**Current.** Closes Volume II; opens Volume III by saying the language of mathematics is now pointed at the simplest physical thing, force.

**Status.** Stable. No revision queued.

## III → IV  (Force into Energy)

**Current text.**

> Force times distance is energy; force across time is impulse.
> We have learned why things stand and why they move.
> The next volume follows the energy that makes them do it: heat, work, electromagnetism, light, the conserved currency of physics.

**Proposed revision.** Acknowledge the Force/Energy/Matter unity that the current text leaves implicit. The three volumes are one continuous body of classical physics presented from three vantages, not three new domains in sequence. The interlude should say so without lecturing.

> Force times distance is energy; force across time is impulse.
> What changes between the book just closed and the book that opens is not the physics but the vantage. We have been working through one classical body, mechanics shading into thermodynamics shading into electromagnetism shading into materials, and we will continue through it for the next two books. The reader who treats Volumes III, IV, and V as three subjects rather than three perspectives on one is reading harder than necessary.
> Now: heat, work, electromagnetism, light. The conserved currency of the physical world.

**Rationale.** Reviewer-honesty. A working physicist reading the current interlude sees an artificial seam. The revision admits the seam is editorial, not physical.

**Status.** Queued. Not applied.

## IV → V  (Energy into Matter)

**Current text.**

> Energy moves through matter, and matter is what makes energy local, accountable, and useful.
> The next volume asks what things are made of, why their properties are what they are, and how the engineer chooses material for purpose.

**Proposed revision.** Continue the unity acknowledgement begun in the III → IV revision; close the three-volume classical body and surface the layer underneath.

> Energy moves through matter; matter is what makes energy local, accountable, and useful. We close the three-volume classical body with the layer underneath both: atoms, bonds, the materials they make, and the failure modes that follow from how those materials are put together.
> The reader who has been carrying the unity will find that what was called Hooke's law in Volume III, specific heat in Volume IV, and elastic constants in Volume V are the same phenomenon read through three different mathematical languages.

**Rationale.** Closes the loop opened in III → IV. The reader now has explicit permission to see the three volumes as one.

**Status.** Queued. Not applied.

## V → VI  (Matter into Life)

**Current.** Closes Volume V; opens Volume VI by saying living matter is matter that maintains itself, learns, evolves, and dies on its own schedule.

**Status.** Stable. No revision queued. The current text already acknowledges that biology is not a new physical category but a way matter behaves at scale.

## VI → VII  (Life into Information)

**Current.** Closes Volume VI; opens Volume VII by saying the next volume is the parallel thread: information as structure, representation, control, and constraint, treated as engineering. Reader is told the two ascents (physical and informational) meet at Volume VIII.

**Status.** Stable. No revision queued. This interlude already does the parallelism work that the III → IV revision is queued to do for classical physics.

## Open editorial notes

- The III → IV and IV → V revisions are paired. They land together, or not at all. Applying only one breaks the rhetoric of the pair.
- The VI → VII interlude already does the parallel-thread work. After the III/IV/V pair lands, consider whether VI → VII should be lightly revised so its "parallel thread" framing visibly matches the III → IV "one body" framing. Not currently planned; flagged as a possible third revision.
- An interlude should never carry technical content the chapter does not also carry. If a revision tempts us into smuggling content into an interlude, the chapter is the wrong place for that content.
- Length budget: each interlude stays under ~150 words. The III → IV revision (~110 words) and IV → V revision (~75 words) both clear the budget.
