# Volume IV: Energy

**Working chapter count**: 14
**Working page total**: ~1,440
**Half-life of the contents**: durable. The thermodynamic and electromagnetic core does not age; the energy-systems chapter (12) is medium-life.

## Scope

Energy is the conserved currency of physics, and the book that names it as such. The reader takes Volume III's mechanical energy and extends it through thermodynamics, heat transfer, electromagnetism, optics, and quantum effects relevant to engineering. By the end the reader can write energy balances across mechanical, thermal, chemical, electrical, and optical processes; reason about efficiency limits; and understand why every engineered system is, at the end, a power balance with losses.

The book's depth standard from settled question 9: the reader can model heat exchangers, AC circuits, antennas, waveguides, and energy-conversion machines well enough to design and diagnose them. The reader does not leave able to derive thermodynamics from statistical mechanics from first principles; that remains a graduate-physics specialisation.

## Arc within the book

Chapters 1-4 cover thermodynamics: laws, equations of state, heat engines, entropy. Chapters 5-6 are heat transfer and thermal systems. Chapter 7 closes transport phenomena with mass transfer and separation processes, since chemical, environmental, food, and pharmaceutical engineering all depend on it and it had no home before. Chapters 8-10 are electromagnetism: Maxwell's equations, circuits, waves and antennas. Chapter 11 is optics. Chapter 12 covers engineering quantum mechanics (lasers, semiconductors, photovoltaics, devices). Chapter 13 covers plasma physics for engineers, the substrate of fusion, etching, propulsion, and arcs. Chapter 14 closes the volume by integrating energy systems: generation, conversion, storage, distribution.

## Substantial book project

The reader designs and characterises an energy system: a solar dryer, a small heat pump, a Stirling engine, a parabolic concentrator, or equivalent. Includes thermodynamic analysis, heat-transfer calculations, electrical-system specification, and measured efficiency.
