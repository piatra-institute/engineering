# Volume V: Matter

**Working chapter count**: 12
**Working page total**: ~1,200
**Half-life of the contents**: durable. Bonding, phase diagrams, materials classes are foundational. Specific commercial alloys and polymers are medium-life and tagged accordingly.

## Scope

The book covers what things are made of, why their properties are what they are, and how to choose the right material for a given engineered purpose. The thread runs from atomic bonding through phase diagrams to the major materials classes (metals, polymers, ceramics, composites), through degradation (corrosion, fatigue, wear), to materials selection and the manufacturing-driven side of material properties.

The depth standard from settled question 9: the reader can specify a material for a structural, thermal, electrical, or chemical application; predict failure modes; and read a materials data sheet without being deceived. The reader does not leave able to do crystallographic work or solid-state physics from first principles.

## Arc within the book

Chapter 1 introduces atoms and bonds. Chapter 2 covers reactions and equilibria, and turns the chemistry into thermodynamic accounting that connects back to Volume IV. Chapter 3 covers solutions and electrochemistry, with batteries as the integrative example. Chapter 4 introduces phase diagrams, the central organising tool of materials science. Chapters 5-8 cover the four materials classes: metals, polymers, ceramics, composites. Chapters 9-10 cover degradation and the materials-selection process. Chapters 11-12 close with the manufacturing-materials interaction and a short chapter on energetic materials (combustion, explosives, propellants).

## Substantial book project

The reader is given a structural function (a small bracket, a vessel, a component under load and temperature) and produces an end-to-end materials-selection report: requirements, property charts, candidate down-selection, supplier-data review, processing route, expected failure modes, predicted service life, sustainability footprint. Reviewed against a public rubric.
