**Working chapter count**: 13
**Working page total**: ~1,200
**Half-life of the contents**: foundational core (cells, metabolism, biomechanics) is durable; chapters 9-10 (synthetic biology, biomanufacturing) are medium-life; Chapter 12 is medium-life and tagged accordingly.

## Scope

Biology as engineering's encounter with self-organising matter. The reader treats living systems as engineered objects: bounded, layered, controlled, error-correcting, evolving, failure-prone, and built of the materials of Volumes IV-V. The standard from Q9 is engineering depth: the reader can model bioenergetics, biomechanics, transport across membranes, population dynamics, and tissue mechanics. The reader can read a biological data sheet, design a bioreactor, specify a biocompatible material, and reason about failure modes in living systems.

The reader does not leave able to write a molecular-biology graduate exam. The point of the book is the engineering frame, not disciplinary completeness in life science.

The book also carries a load-bearing claim from settled question 38: living matter is "self-maintaining, evolved, adaptive, failure-prone." That frame is the reading instruction throughout.

## Arc within the book

Chapter 1 introduces cells as engineered objects. Chapters 2-4 cover the cell's energy, information, and machinery. Chapter 5 scales up to tissues and organs. Chapter 6 covers biomechanics. Chapter 7 covers neural and immune systems as control systems. Chapter 8 scales further to populations and ecology. Chapters 9-10 cover synthetic biology and biomanufacturing. Chapter 11 covers bioinformatics: sequence, structure, and omics, placed here so the cross-cut between Volumes VI and VII has its own home rather than being buried inside biomanufacturing or distributed software. Chapter 12 covers biocompatibility and medical devices. Chapter 13 closes with regenerative medicine and the morphogenetic-control thread that connects to the institute's CERB work.

## Substantial book project

The reader takes a biological process (fermentation, plant growth, blood pressure regulation, immune response to a vaccine) and produces an engineering analysis: bounded model, parameter identification from literature, simulation, prediction, comparison with measured data where available. The deliverable demonstrates that biology yields to the same accounting discipline as the rest of engineering, while honouring its non-linearity and feedback complexity.

## Bridges to adjacent books
