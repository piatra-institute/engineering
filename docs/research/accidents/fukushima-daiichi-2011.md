# Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Accident, 2011

## Date(s) and location

The accident initiated on 11 March 2011 following the magnitude 9.0 T\=ohoku earthquake and tsunami. The plant is on the Pacific coast of Fukushima Prefecture, Japan, operated by Tokyo Electric Power Company (TEPCO).

## Technical mechanism

The earthquake triggered automatic SCRAM (rapid shutdown) of operating units 1, 2, and 3; offsite power was lost. The subsequent tsunami over-topped the plant's $5.7$-m seawall with a $\sim 14$-m wave; the backup diesel generators and seawater cooling pumps located at low elevation were submerged and disabled. The resulting station blackout meant the reactor decay heat could not be removed; the cores of units 1, 2, and 3 progressively overheated, melted, and breached the reactor pressure vessels; hydrogen produced by zirconium-water reaction accumulated and exploded in units 1, 3, and 4. Substantial radioactive release occurred over the days following the initiating event; the offsite contamination required evacuation of approximately $160{,}000$ people.

## Organisational / regulatory mechanism

The pre-event tsunami-design basis was based on historical Pacific-coast tsunamis and underestimated the plausible magnitude for the Tohoku region. The seawall and the placement of safety-critical equipment at low elevation reflected the under-estimation. Internal TEPCO assessments and external academic studies in the years preceding the accident had identified the under-estimation but did not lead to operational changes. Post-accident investigations (TEPCO 2012; the Japanese Diet's Nuclear Accident Independent Investigation Commission; IAEA reports) documented both technical and institutional contributing factors.

## Short-form summaries

A $\sim 14$-m tsunami inundated the Fukushima Daiichi plant on 11 March 2011, disabling backup diesel generators in three operating reactor units and producing station blackouts that led to core damage and offsite release of radioactive material across approximately $1{,}000\,\text{km}^2$.

On 11 March 2011 a magnitude-9.0 earthquake and the subsequent tsunami exceeded the design basis of the Fukushima Daiichi nuclear plant, submerged the backup diesel generators, and produced a station blackout in three operating units; the resulting core damage and hydrogen explosions released substantial radioactivity offsite, requiring evacuation of approximately $160{,}000$ residents and triggering a multi-year decommissioning programme.
