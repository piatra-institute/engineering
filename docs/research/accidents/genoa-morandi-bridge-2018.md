# Genoa Morandi Bridge, 2018

## Date(s) and location

The collapse occurred at approximately 11:36 local time on 14 August 2018 on the Polcevera Viaduct (popularly known as Ponte Morandi), an elevated section of the A10 motorway crossing the Polcevera river valley in Genoa, Italy \cite{acc:mit-morandi-2018}. The collapsed span was the section supported by Pylon 9, approximately 210 m of deck plus the pylon itself. The collapse killed 43 people (motorists on the bridge; people in the warehouses beneath the bridge) and destroyed multiple commercial and residential buildings. The bridge had been in service since 1967 and was a critical link in the Italian motorway network connecting the Ligurian coast with the Po Valley.

## Technical mechanism

The Italian Ministry of Infrastructure's Inspection Commission identified the proximate mechanism as failure of the post-tensioning stays on the southern side of Pylon 9 \cite{acc:mit-morandi-2018}. The Morandi-design bridge used a hybrid cable-stayed system in which the main stays consisted of post-tensioned concrete-encased tendons (steel tendons embedded in pre-stressed concrete sheaths, with the concrete intended both to provide compression for the post-tensioning and to protect the tendons from corrosion). The design was unusual; it differed from conventional cable-stayed bridges (where the stays are exposed steel cables) and from conventional pre-stressed concrete bridges (where the post-tensioning is internal to the concrete deck rather than carrying the deck's load through external stays).

The investigation identified that the stay tendons on Pylon 9 had experienced progressive corrosion over decades, with section loss accumulating in the steel tendons inside the concrete encasement. The corrosion was difficult to detect by surface inspection (the concrete encasement hid the tendon condition) and was difficult to quantify by non-destructive testing of the available technologies at the time of inspection. The bridge's location in coastal Genoa exposed it to salt-laden marine air; its location adjacent to the city's industrial zone exposed it to industrial atmospheric corrosion; the cumulative environmental exposure was severe relative to the design's protective margin.

The investigation found that the immediate failure was the rupture of one of the southern stays on Pylon 9, after which the load redistribution to the remaining stays exceeded their reduced capacity, and the cascade of stay failures led to collapse of the pylon and the deck it supported. The cascade was rapid; the deck did not warn occupants before collapse.

## Organisational / regulatory mechanism

The Ministry of Infrastructure investigation identified contributing factors at multiple organisational levels \cite{acc:mit-morandi-2018}. The bridge's concession operator (Autostrade per l'Italia, ASPI, the operating company) had conducted inspections that had identified deterioration of the stays; the inspections had not produced an action plan to address the deterioration on a timescale that would have prevented the failure. The operator's contracted inspection consultants had recommended more aggressive intervention; the operator's internal decision-making had not endorsed the recommendations. The regulatory authority (the Ministry of Infrastructure and the related concession-supervision structures) had not compelled the operator to act on the inspection findings.

The post-accident response included: prosecution of the concession operator's senior executives and the regulatory officials in the supervisory chain; the revocation of ASPI's concession over the bridge (and a re-negotiation of the broader Italian motorway concession framework); the demolition of the remaining sections of the bridge and the construction of a replacement bridge (the Genoa San Giorgio Bridge, designed by Renzo Piano, opened in 2020); and the broader Italian regulatory recognition that the inspection and intervention regime for cable-stayed bridges of the Morandi era required substantial overhaul.

## Short-form summaries

\textbf{One sentence}: A 210-m section of the Polcevera Viaduct (Ponte Morandi) in Genoa, Italy, collapsed on 14 August 2018, killing 43 people; the failure originated in corrosion-degraded post-tensioning stays on one of the three cable-stayed pylons, with contributing factors including the bridge's unique structural design, aggressive marine and industrial environment, decades of deferred maintenance and contested inspection findings, and the concession operator's contested response to prior warnings.

\textbf{One paragraph}: The Morandi Bridge's hybrid cable-stayed design used post-tensioned concrete-encased steel tendons as the main stays, a design that was unusual at construction (1967) and that had become harder to inspect and harder to maintain as the bridge aged. The southern stays of Pylon 9 had experienced progressive corrosion in the bridge's marine and industrial atmosphere; the corrosion was difficult to detect by non-destructive testing of the available technologies and was the subject of contested inspection findings and contested recommendations for intervention. On 14 August 2018, the deteriorated stays ruptured; the load redistribution exceeded the remaining stays' capacity; the pylon and 210 m of deck collapsed, killing 43 people. The investigation identified failures in the concession operator's response to inspection findings, in the regulator's enforcement of the inspection regime, and in the broader political-economic environment that had deferred substantial intervention on a critical piece of national infrastructure.
