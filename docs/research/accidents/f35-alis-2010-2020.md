# F-35 Autonomic Logistics Information System programme, 2010-2020

## Date(s) and location

The ALIS programme spanned approximately 2010-2020 across F-35 partner-nation military operators and the prime contractor (Lockheed Martin) and the US Department of Defense F-35 Joint Program Office.

## Technical mechanism

ALIS was conceived as a comprehensive logistics-management software for the F-35 fleet, integrating maintenance management, supply chain, mission planning, performance monitoring, and fleet-wide data analysis. The technical development encountered the typical large-software-programme difficulties at scale: requirements drift across multi-generation hardware and operational concepts; integration-and-test challenges across diverse operational environments; software-quality issues that produced chronic operational deficiencies; user-experience problems that reduced adoption by maintenance personnel; data-management and cybersecurity challenges in the multi-nation deployment.

## Organisational / regulatory mechanism

The programme was managed within the F-35 Joint Program Office's broader procurement structure, with the prime contractor responsible for delivery and the partner nations as user organisations. The procurement and acquisition regulatory regime constrained iteration speed and feedback cycles; the requirements management across the multi-decade programme produced substantial accumulated technical debt. The GAO and other oversight bodies documented the programme's chronic difficulties across multiple reports. The eventual decision to develop a replacement (ODIN) in approximately 2020 acknowledged that the existing system could not be incrementally repaired to meet the operational requirement.

## Short-form summaries

The F-35 Autonomic Logistics Information System (ALIS), the comprehensive logistics-management software for the F-35 fleet, was substantially over budget, late, and operationally problematic across more than a decade of development; the eventual decision to replace it with ODIN (Operational Data Integrated Network) acknowledged the programme's accumulated difficulties.

The F-35 ALIS programme (approximately 2010-2020) is the contemporary defence-software case: more than a decade of attempted delivery of a fleet-wide logistics-management system encountered the chronic large-software-programme failure modes, accumulated technical debt the procurement regime could not unblock, and was eventually superseded by a fresh-start effort (ODIN).
