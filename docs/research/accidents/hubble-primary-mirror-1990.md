# Hubble Space Telescope primary mirror, 1990

## Date(s) and location

The Hubble Space Telescope was launched aboard Space Shuttle Discovery (STS-31) on 24 April 1990 from Kennedy Space Center. Imaging anomalies were identified during the first weeks of on-orbit operation. The primary mirror had been fabricated by Perkin-Elmer Corporation between 1979 and 1981 in Danbury, Connecticut. Optical correction was eventually delivered by COSTAR (Corrective Optics Space Telescope Axial Replacement), installed during the STS-61 servicing mission in December 1993.

## Technical mechanism

The NASA Hubble Space Telescope Optical Systems Failure Report, prepared by the Optical Systems Board of Investigation chaired by Lew Allen Jr.\ and released in November 1990, identified the cause as a misalignment in the reflective null corrector (RNC), the optical-test instrument used to measure the mirror's figure during polishing. A field lens within the RNC was mispositioned along the optical axis by approximately $1.3 \,\si{\milli\meter}$. The mispositioning resulted from a misinterpretation of a measurement target on a metering rod used during RNC assembly: the end cap and the target feature were not properly distinguished, and the spacing was set against the wrong feature. The error in the test instrument propagated into the polished mirror's figure: the mirror's outer edge was about $2 \,\si{\micro\meter}$ too flat, an error large enough to ruin diffraction-limited imaging across the optical bandpass. The error was not the mirror's; it was the test instrument's, and it was undetectable by the test the test instrument was designed to perform \cite{acc:nasa-hubble-optical-systems-1990}.

Two auxiliary optical tests, a refractive null corrector and an inverse null test, had indicated discrepancies during fabrication. The refractive test, designed for low-frequency figure measurement, showed a result inconsistent with the RNC's reading. The inverse null test similarly indicated a discrepancy. Both were set aside under the assumption that the RNC was the more authoritative instrument.

## Organisational / regulatory mechanism

The Allen Commission found that the primary mechanism of the failure was a flawed manufacturing process at Perkin-Elmer. The flaw was not a single mistake by a single individual; it was a process that allowed a single test instrument to be the only authoritative figure measurement, that did not require independent verification of the test instrument itself, that allowed cost and schedule pressure to override the auxiliary tests' anomalies, and that did not give NASA's quality-assurance representation effective oversight of the optical-test setup. The report recommended that future optical-fabrication programmes require independent verification of test instruments, formal anomaly-resolution procedures that cannot be informally overridden, and engaged customer oversight of the test apparatus rather than only of the test results \cite{acc:nasa-hubble-optical-systems-1990}.
