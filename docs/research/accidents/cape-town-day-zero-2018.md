# Cape Town Day Zero Water Crisis, 2018

## Date(s) and location

The drought spanned 2015-2018. ``Day Zero'' (the projected date at which the municipal water-supply system would have insufficient water to supply the network and would have required emergency rationing distribution at collection points) was projected variously between April and August 2018 across the early months of 2018; effective demand-reduction averted the day.

## Technical mechanism

The Western Cape's water supply depends substantially on dam-storage in mountainous regions east of the city. The 2015-2018 drought produced consecutive years of below-average winter precipitation, leaving dam levels substantially depleted by early 2018. The supply system's design basis had been calibrated to the historical precipitation record; the multi-year drought exceeded the design basis in severity. Emergency response combined aggressive demand-reduction (administrative tariffs; visible-public-pressure campaign; reduction of per-capita use from $\sim 220$ L/day to $\sim 100$ L/day at peak restriction), emergency supply (rapid deployment of small-scale desalination; additional groundwater development), and operational changes (release-rule modifications; pressure management).

## Organisational / regulatory mechanism

The Western Cape provincial government and the City of Cape Town municipal government coordinated the emergency response with national government and industry stakeholders. The pre-drought planning had anticipated climate-driven water stress in long-term planning documents but had not implemented the supply-and-demand diversification at the pace the climate trajectory required. The post-event reassessment included revisions to the design basis for climate-sensitive water systems.

## Short-form summaries

A multi-year drought from 2015 brought Cape Town's water-supply system to within months of running out of water in early 2018, requiring emergency demand-reduction that cut per-capita use by approximately $50\%$ and emergency supply development.

The 2015-2018 Western Cape drought exceeded the climate basis of the Cape Town water-supply system; the city avoided ``Day Zero'' through aggressive demand-reduction and emergency supply development, illustrating both the climate-mismatched-design failure mode and the effectiveness of an integrated emergency response.
