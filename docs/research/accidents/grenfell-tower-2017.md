# Grenfell Tower Fire, 2017

## Date(s) and location

The fire occurred on the night of 14 June 2017 at Grenfell Tower, a $24$-storey residential tower block in North Kensington, West London, operated by the Kensington and Chelsea Tenant Management Organisation on behalf of the Royal Borough of Kensington and Chelsea.

## Technical mechanism

A fire originated in a refrigerator in a flat on the fourth floor. The fire breached the flat's window and ignited the building's exterior cladding, an aluminium-composite material with a polyethylene core that had been installed during a 2015-2016 refurbishment. The polyethylene core acted as a fuel; the cladding system created vertical air channels that promoted upward flame spread; the fire spread to the top of the building over $\sim 15$ minutes. The internal compartmentation that the building's ``stay-put'' fire-safety policy assumed was breached. $72$ residents died; $74$ were injured.

## Organisational / regulatory mechanism

The Grenfell Tower Inquiry (Phase 1 reporting 2019; Phase 2 reporting 2024) documented systemic failures across the building-products certification regime, the building-control regulatory regime, the contractor selection and procurement for the refurbishment, the cladding-manufacturer testing-and-marketing, the local authority management, the London Fire Brigade's response, and the broader regulatory environment. The cladding-system had been tested in configurations that did not include the as-installed assembly; the regulatory framework permitted the gap. The refurbishment procurement had selected the lower-cost cladding option. The fire brigade's initial response followed the stay-put advice that the building's fire-engineering had assumed; the change to evacuation came too late for many residents.

## Short-form summaries

A kitchen fire in a flat at Grenfell Tower in West London on 14 June 2017 spread rapidly up the building's flammable aluminium-composite cladding, killing $72$ residents in one of the deadliest UK residential fires of the modern period.

The Grenfell Tower fire of 14 June 2017 illustrates the cascading failure across building-products certification, building-control regulation, refurbishment procurement, contractor practice, and emergency response: a kitchen fire became a building-scale catastrophe because flammable cladding had been installed under a certification regime that did not catch the installed-assembly's behaviour and a procurement regime that selected lower-cost cladding options.
